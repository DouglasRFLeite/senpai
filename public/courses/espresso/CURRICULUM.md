# Curriculum — Brewing Better Espresso

_Designed by /design-course (Pedagogue × Mission-Achiever), 2026-07-08. /teach
follows this sequence; deviations are recorded in learning-records._

## Learning arc

The learner starts able to pull *a* shot and ends able to dial in any fresh bag
in under five shots. The arc runs taste-first (what does wrong taste like?),
then gives the two levers that fix it (ratio, then grind-vs-time), then welds
everything into one repeatable dialing protocol — theory never appears without
a same-morning use for it.

## Units

### Unit 1: The Brew Ratio
**/teach covers:** dose → beverage-weight ratios (1:1.5–1:2.5), weighing the cup
not the portafilter, how ratio moves strength vs. extraction. (Shipped as
lesson `0001-the-ratio.html`.)
**/author-bank drills:** topic "Brew ratio" — MC on ratio arithmetic and
weighing technique; distractors are the real confusions (ristretto/lungo
weights, crema myths).
**Why here:** ratio is the only variable you can fix by decision rather than
iteration, so it must be automatic before dialing starts.

### Unit 2: Dialing In a New Bag
**/teach covers:** the taste triangle (sour-fast / bitter-slow), grind as the
lever that moves shot time at fixed dose+ratio, one-change-per-shot discipline.
(Shipped as lesson `0002-dialing-in.html`.)
**/author-bank drills:** topic "Dialing in" — MC on the fault→adjustment map;
open questions where the learner diagnoses a described shot and prescribes the
next change (criteria credit: names the extraction fault, links it to taste,
proposes the single correct adjustment, holds dose/ratio fixed).
**Why here:** it composes Unit 1's fixed ratio with the grind lever — the
mission's core skill, placed as soon as its two prerequisites exist.

### Unit 3: The Five-Shot Protocol (to author)
**/teach covers:** a written dialing protocol (shot 1 baseline coarse → bisect
by taste+time), shot logging, when temperature/dose become worth touching.
**/author-bank drills:** mixed-topic Exam over Units 1–3; open questions walk a
full dial-in from a described bag (criteria per protocol step).
**Why here:** interleaves the earlier units under time pressure — this is the
mission's "under five shots" bullet made testable, and the retention pass over
everything before it.

## Deliberation notes

### Consensus
Taste-first sequencing; one variable changed per shot as a course-wide
discipline; puck prep, milk and machine mods stay out of scope (mission's
morning-cup reality, and cognitive-load ceiling).

### Resolved tensions
- Mission-Achiever wanted extraction theory cut entirely; Pedagogue kept a
  minimal sour/bitter model because the fault→fix map is unlearnable as rote —
  settled on "theory only at the moment it explains a taste".
- Pedagogue wanted a spaced review unit; settled on Unit 3's Exam doubling as
  the interleaved retention pass instead of a fourth unit.

### Open questions for the user
- Does your machine have shot-time display or do we teach counting from first
  drip? (Changes Unit 2's protocol wording.)
- Should Unit 3 include a printable log sheet as a reference asset?
